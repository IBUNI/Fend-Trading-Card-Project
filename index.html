Siberian Tiger

Siberian tiger is the rarest species of tiger living in the wild

  • Scientific Name:Panthera tigris altaica
  • Geographical Range:Russia, China, North Korea
  • Habitat:Forests, bush-covered mountains
  • Conservation Status:Endangered

Siberian Tiger is the largest of all of the wild cats in the world. Many are found in the far east of Russia. They average 116 inches in length. Siberian Tigers eat bucks, wild pigs and birds. It is eastimated that there are close to 500 Siberian Tiges in existence.
